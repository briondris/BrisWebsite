About
Brianna Ondris is a web and Unity developer, human-centered designer, and an artist born in the United States. She grew up in northern New Jersey and in 2013 moved to Virginia to pursue an art degree from Virginia Commonwealth University in the Kinetic Imaging department. Today Ondris works as a freelancer developer and artist in Washington D.C.
I create interactive experiences that invite you to play at the edges of the unknown. I am driven by a deep curiosity about seeing into different perspectives and to find the unseen choreographed patterns that make up the fantastic rituals in the everyday world. In my work, I explore digital puppets, human patterns, feminity, and cyborgs. The work I make and the shows I have curated, I continue to push the interaction between the digital space and our own to lead the viewer into the uncharted territory where something truly human can unfold between the two worlds.
In a past life, I worked solely as a full-stack developer for a nonprofit start-up to an insurance corporation. I found then that the most joyful parts of my work were when I was developing and taking risks to include better UI and UX in the projects I was assigned to. I pulled myself out of this world and began a new, dedicating my skills to research funned work and teaching. Through my experiences I have learned an overarching variety of skills, but most importantly that my place in the working world is to be the translation between the functionality a program and the art and design of how it is to be used.
